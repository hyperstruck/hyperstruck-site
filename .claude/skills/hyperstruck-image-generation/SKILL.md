# Hyperstruck Image Generation

Use this skill when generating images or image prompts that should feel consistent with the Hyperstruck visual direction.

## Core Visual System

Default to a premium cinematic editorial aesthetic:

- 16:9 cinematic editorial illustration.
- Deep navy, charcoal, and black foundation.
- Soft neon blue accents used sparingly.
- Warm amber or gold highlights for the focal subject.
- High contrast lighting with controlled shadows.
- Reflective glass, polished dark surfaces, metal, water, or other glossy materials when they fit the concept.
- Sharp realistic details with controlled depth of field.
- Sophisticated, intelligent, slightly tense mood.
- Clean slide-ready composition with negative space for title text.

The visual world should feel premium, restrained, and atmospheric, not generic sci-fi or stock-art.

## Composition

- Favor one strong central metaphor or subject over many competing ideas.
- Keep the background dark and quiet so the focal subject carries the story.
- Use glow, reflection, silhouette, and depth to create drama.
- Leave generous negative space on the left, top-left, or upper third when the image may be used as a slide.
- Make any readable text sparse, short, and intentional.
- Avoid visual clutter even when the concept is complex.

## Colour And Light

- Base palette: deep navy, near-black, charcoal, and muted slate.
- Accent palette: electric blue, cyan glow, warm amber, and gold.
- Use warm light to draw attention and cool light to build atmosphere.
- Keep saturation controlled. The image should feel rich, not loud.
- Use reflections and rim light to separate the subject from the background.
- Avoid bright rainbow palettes, flat lighting, and washed-out grey backgrounds.

## Texture And Materials

Use material cues to make images feel tangible:

- Glass, water, polished stone, dark wood, brushed metal, soft fabric, smoke, mist, dust, or atmospheric haze.
- Subtle scratches, fingerprints, condensation, particles, or surface reflections when useful.
- Crisp foreground details with softer background falloff.
- Practical light sources, neon edges, reflected highlights, and cinematic bloom.

## Prompt Template

Use this as the base prompt and adapt the subject:

```text
Create a 16:9 cinematic editorial illustration. Subject: [specific metaphor, scene, or object].

Use the established Hyperstruck visual world: deep navy, charcoal, and black foundation; soft neon blue accents; warm amber/gold focal highlights; high contrast lighting; reflective or polished materials; sharp realistic details; and controlled depth of field.

The image should feel intelligent, restrained, atmospheric, and sophisticated rather than cartoonish. Build the scene around one clear visual metaphor. Use glow, reflection, silhouette, shadow, and depth to create drama without clutter.

Keep the composition clean and slide-ready, with generous negative space for title text. Avoid logos, real brand names, cluttered text, flat vector art, overly bright colors, generic sci-fi, and unreadable busy layouts.
```

## Avoid

- Generic cyberpunk cityscapes.
- Overcrowded labels or decorative text.
- Real company logos or tool names.
- Bright rainbow palettes.
- Flat vector art unless explicitly requested.
- Cartoonish or toy-like rendering unless explicitly requested.
- Images that look like stock art or flat vector illustrations.

## Quality Check

Before generating, confirm the prompt has:

1. A clear metaphor or visual subject.
2. The dark navy / black / neon blue / warm amber palette.
3. Reflective glass or polished material cues.
4. Controlled contrast, depth, and atmospheric lighting.
5. Slide-ready negative space.
6. Minimal text with only short readable labels.
